Hidden Cash Flow Dynamics That Destroy Small Solar Companies:

have a BRUTAL business model that most retail investors don't appreciate:

The Working Capital Nightmare:
1. PROJECT-BASED REVENUE CYCLE:
   - Win tender/contract (Month 0)
   - Purchase equipment (Month 1-2): PAY suppliers upfront
   - Installation (Month 2-4): PAY labor and subcontractors
   - Customer payment (Month 5-8): RECEIVE payment in installments
   - Cash outflow PRECEDES cash inflow by 3-6 months

2. WORKING CAPITAL TRAP:
   - Every new project requires upfront cash
   - Small companies like JS Solar lack capital reserves
   - Forced to rely on:
     * Bank facilities (expensive, require collateral)
     * Supplier credit (limited for small players)
     * Customer deposits (hard to negotiate)
   - Growth actually WORSENS cash flow (paradox of growth)

3. THE QUARTERLY REPORTING DISTORTION:
   - Revenue recognized on percentage-of-completion or project completion
   - Quarter with multiple project completions = high revenue, profit
   - Quarter with projects in progress = low revenue, losses

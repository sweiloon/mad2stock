renewable projects.

OPPORTUNITY: Petronas's "Net Zero 2050" commitment includes RM 25-30 billion
for renewable energy infrastructure by 2030. Vantris's offshore engineering
expertise positions it for offshore wind and floating solar projects.

2. BANK NEGARA MALAYSIA POLICY IMPACT
BNM's Overnight Policy Rate (OPR): Currently at 3.00%
- Maintained steady rate despite global volatility
- Stable rates support corporate borrowing for projects
- Ringgit stability (RM/USD 4.40-4.70 range) aids cost predictability
- Low inflation (2.0-2.5%) supports project economics

VANTRIS BENEFIT: Post-restructuring, company needs access to working capital
financing. Stable BNM policy makes trade financing and bank guarantees more
accessible for project bidding.

Malaysia's National Energy Policy (2023-2035):
- Target: 70% renewable energy by 2050
- Offshore wind potential: 60 GW in Malaysian waters
- Decommissioning fund: RM 5-8 billion estimated for next 10 years
- Gas hub strategy: Position Malaysia as regional LNG trading center

STRATEGIC FIT: Vantris's vessels and fabrication facilities can support:
- Offshore wind turbine installation
- Platform decommissioning (40+ aging platforms)
- LNG infrastructure expansion
- Cross-border energy projects (ASEAN grid integration)

MALAYSIA-SPECIFIC RISKS
------------------------------------------------------------

RISK 1: PETRONAS PAYMENT TERMS
Malaysian oil & gas projects notorious for:
- Long payment cycles (90-120 days standard)
- Variation order disputes delaying payments
- Progress billing verification bureaucracy

IMPACT ON VANTRIS: Cash flow management critical. Working capital needs higher
than revenue suggests. Post-restructuring liquidity must be monitored.

RISK 2: MALAYSIAN RINGGIT VOLATILITY
Energy projects priced in USD but costs in MYR creates currency exposure:
- USD revenue conversion risk if MYR strengthens
- Equipment/material imports vulnerable to MYR weakness
- Natural hedge limited as fuel costs also MYR-denominated

CURRENT SITUATION: MYR at RM 4.40-4.70/USD (historically weak) actually
BENEFITS Vantris short-term as USD contracts convert to higher MYR revenue.

RISK 3: MALAYSIAN SKILLED LABOR SHORTAGE
Energy sector facing:
- Aging workforce (avg age 45+ in offshore roles)
- Youth preference for tech/finance over oil & gas
- Foreign worker restrictions post-COVID
- Salary inflation for critical skills (15-20% annually for subsea engineers)

VANTRIS CHALLENGE: Rebuilding technical teams after restructuring layoffs.
Reputation damage makes talent attraction harder. May need premium salaries.

Petronas mandates that contractors develop local Malaysian vendors and technology:
- VDP (Vendor Development Program) gives preference to local companies
- Vantris qualifies for VDP benefits post-restructuring
- Can partner with foreign technology providers as local executing partner
- Access to Petronas technology transfer programs

STRATEGIC MOVE: Position as "local champion" for foreign offshore wind
developers needing Malaysian partners for Petronas projects.

Sarawak & Sabah experiencing infrastructure surge:
- Sarawak's 12th State Plan: RM 315 billion (2021-2025)
- Sabah's coastal development programs
- Trans-Borneo energy connectivity projects
- LNG export terminal expansions

VANTRIS ADVANTAGE: Existing fabrication yard in Kuala Terengganu can support
East Malaysia projects. Vessel fleet can relocate to Sabah/Sarawak waters for
higher margins (less competition than Peninsular Malaysia).

OPPORTUNITY 3: ASEAN REGIONAL INTEGRATION
Malaysia's role in ASEAN Power Grid and Trans-ASEAN Gas Pipeline:
- Cross-border subsea pipeline projects

POTENTIAL: Vantris could secure roles in ASEAN-level projects where Malaysian
government backing gives competitive advantage over purely foreign contractors.

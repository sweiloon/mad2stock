INSIGHT 2: The 1-Sen Death Spiral Psychology
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Trading at RM 0.010 isn't just a low price - it's a psychological and
structural death trap that retail investors underestimate:

Mathematical Reality:
• At 1 sen, the stock can only move in 100% increments (0.5 sen down = -50%)
• Minimum trade size: 100,000 shares = RM 1,000 investment
• Daily volume likely under RM 10,000 (less than 10 average trades/day)
• Bid-ask spread often 50-100% (0.005 bid, 0.010 ask)

Professional Insight:
Penny stock specialists know that 1-sen stocks have hidden friction costs:

Example: You buy 500,000 shares at RM 0.010 = RM 5,000 investment
- Stock rises 100% to RM 0.015 = RM 2,500 paper gain (+50%)
- But to sell 500,000 shares at 1-sen level requires finding buyer
- Actual exit might be RM 0.010 or RM 0.005 due to no liquidity
- Real gain: RM 0 to RM -2,500 (loss) despite 50% price increase

The "1-Sen Paradox": Even if company recovers, shareholders can't exit
profitably due to structural illiquidity. Professional traders avoid or only
play with tiny speculation amounts (<0.5% portfolio).
